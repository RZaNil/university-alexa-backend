EWU CSE Program - Complete Course & Curriculum Guide

## Program Overview

### Total Credit Requirements
The total requirement for the CSE program is 140 credits.

================================
CREDIT BREAKDOWN BY CATEGORY
================================

## Compulsory Language & GED Courses (9 Credits)
You must complete 9 credits from:
- ENG101 – Basic English (3 credits)
- ENG102 – Composition & Communication Skills (3 credits, requires ENG101)
- GEN226 – Emergence of Bangladesh (3 credits, requires ENG102)

## Elective GED Courses (9 Credits)
9 credits required, including one course from each category:
- Social Science (3 credits)
- Arts/Humanities (3 credits)
- Business (3 credits)

## Science Courses (11 Credits)
- PHY109 – Engineering Physics I (4 credits, requires MAT102)
- PHY209 – Engineering Physics II (3 credits, requires MAT205)
- CHE109 – Engineering Chemistry (4 credits)

## Compulsory Math & Statistics Courses (15 Credits)
- MAT101 – Differential & Integral Calculus (3 credits)
- MAT102 – Differential Equations (3 credits, requires MAT101)
- MAT104 – Coordinate Geometry & Vector Analysis (3 credits, requires MAT101)
- MAT205 – Linear Algebra & Complex Variable (3 credits, requires MAT102)
- STA102 – Statistics & Probability (3 credits, no prerequisite)

## Core CSE Courses (62 Credits)
Core courses include:
- CSE103 – Structured Programming (4.5 credits, no prerequisite)
- CSE106 – Discrete Mathematics (3 credits, requires CSE103)
- CSE110 – Object Oriented Programming (4.5 credits, requires CSE106)
- CSE200 – Computer-Aided Engineering Drawing
- CSE207 – Data Structures (4.5 credits, requires CSE110)
- CSE209 – Electrical Circuits
- CSE246 – Algorithms (3 credits, requires CSE207)
- CSE251 – Electronic Circuits (requires CSE209)
- CSE302 – Database Systems (4.5 credits with lab, requires CSE106)
- CSE325 – Operating Systems (requires CSE207)
- CSE345 – Digital Logic Design (with 1-credit lab, requires CSE251)
- CSE347 – Information System Analysis & Design (requires CSE302)
- CSE350 – Data Communications
- CSE360 – Computer Architecture (theory-only, requires CSE325)
- CSE405 – Computer Networks (requires CSE246)
- CSE487 – Cyber Security, Ethics & Law (requires CSE405)

## Capstone Project (6 Credits)
CSE400 – Capstone Project is 6 credits completed across 3 semesters.

## Major Area Requirements (20 Credits)
- 2 compulsory major courses
- 3 major electives

## Non-Major Electives (8 Credits Minimum)
Minimum 8 credits from non-major elective list including:
- CSE225 – Numerical Methods
- CSE313 – Theory of Computation
- CSE460 – Cryptography
- CSE471 – Compiler Design (requires CSE246)
- CSE483 – Graph Theory
- CSE484 – Computational Geometry

================================
CSE CURRICULUM FAQ
================================

Q: How many credits are required for CSE?
A: Total 140 credits are required for B.Sc. in CSE.

Q: What are the major areas in CSE?
A: There are 4 major areas: Intelligent Systems (Data Science), Software Engineering, Networking, and Hardware Engineering.

Q: What is the prerequisite for CSE366 Artificial Intelligence?
A: CSE246 (Algorithms) is the prerequisite for CSE366.

Q: What is the prerequisite for CSE428 HCI?
A: CSE412 (Software Engineering) is the prerequisite.

Q: Is CSE103 a prerequisite for any course?
A: Yes, CSE103 (Structured Programming) is required for CSE106 (Discrete Math).

Q: How many credits is the Capstone Project?
A: CSE400 Capstone Project is 6 credits, taken over 3 semesters.

Q: Which math courses are compulsory?
A: MAT101, MAT102, MAT104, MAT205, and STA102 are compulsory.

Q: I am a 1st year student, what courses should I take?
A: In your 1st year (freshman year), you cover 3 semesters:

1st Semester:
1. CSE103: Structured Programming (4.5)
2. ENG101: Basic English (3)
3. MAT101: Differential Calculus (3)

2nd Semester:
1. CSE106: Discrete Mathematics (3)
2. ENG102: Composition & Communication (3)
3. MAT102: Differential Equations (3)
4. CHE109: Engineering Chemistry (4)

3rd Semester:
1. CSE110: Object Oriented Programming (4.5)
2. PHY109: Engineering Physics I (4)
3. STA102: Statistics & Probability (3)
4. GEN226: Emergence of Bangladesh (3)

================================
COURSE TOPICS AND CONTENT
================================

## Programming Courses
- CSE103 – Structured Programming: Programming fundamentals, basic syntax
- CSE110 – Object Oriented Programming: OOP concepts, object-oriented design
- CSE207 – Data Structures: Recursion, stack, queue, linked list, binary trees, heaps, hash tables
- CSE246 – Algorithms: BFS, DFS, graph traversal, greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, complexity analysis, shortest-path algorithms
- CSE471 – Compiler Design: Compiler phases and construction

## Systems Courses
- CSE302 – Database Systems: SQL, relational databases, ER diagrams, file organization, data modeling
- CSE325 – Operating Systems: Memory management, scheduling, file systems, disk scheduling, deadlocks, process management
- CSE360 – Computer Architecture: CPU design, instruction set architecture (ISA), pipeline, caching, memory hierarchy
- CSE345 – Digital Logic Design: Boolean algebra, logic gates, flip-flops, counters, registers, digital circuits

## Networking & Security Courses
- CSE405 – Computer Networks: Network layers, TCP/IP, IP addressing, routing, switching, routing algorithms
- CSE487 – Cyber Security, Ethics & Law: Firewalls, ethical hacking, cyber laws, secure communication
- CSE350 – Data Communications: Modulation, error detection, digital communication theory
- CSE453 – Wireless Networks: Wireless transmission and protocols
- CSE457 – Cellular Networks: 4G/5G technologies, mobility management

## AI & Data Science Courses
- CSE366 – Artificial Intelligence: AI foundations, search algorithms
- CSE303 – Statistics for Data Science: Regression, probability, hypothesis testing, essential math for ML
- CSE478 – Machine Learning: Machine learning fundamentals
- CSE474 – Pattern Recognition: Neural networks, pattern classification
- CSE477 – Data Mining: Classification and clustering
- CSE438 – Digital Image Processing: Convolution, filtering, image enhancement
- CSE445 – Computer Vision: Object detection, feature extraction
- CSE488 – Big Data Analytics: Hadoop, Spark, Big Data frameworks

## Hardware & Electronics Courses
- CSE209 – Electrical Circuits: Electrical circuit concepts
- CSE251 – Electronic Circuits: Transistor circuits, amplifiers, diodes, MOSFET operation
- CSE442 – Microprocessors & Microcontrollers: Microcontroller programming
- CSE492 – Robotics: Robotics mechanisms
- CSE494 – Embedded Systems: Embedded systems programming
- CSE446 – ASIC Design Using FPGA: FPGA design

## Software Engineering Courses
- CSE412 – Software Engineering: Agile, Scrum methodologies, requirement analysis, project documentation
- CSE430 – Software Testing & QA: Testing strategies, QA process
- CSE428 – HCI: User Interface (UI) and UX design
- CSE347 – Information System Analysis & Design: UML diagrams, system modeling
- CSE479 – Web Programming: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, backend basics
- CSE489 – Mobile Programming: Android-based mobile app development

## Math & Science Courses
- MAT101 – Calculus: Differential & Integral Calculus
- MAT102 – Differential Equations: Differential equations
- MAT104 – Coordinate Geometry & Vector Analysis: Vector geometry
- MAT205 – Linear Algebra: Linear algebra, complex variables
- STA102 – Statistics & Probability: Probability and data analysis
- PHY109 – Engineering Physics I: Classical mechanics
- PHY209 – Engineering Physics II: Quantum physics basics
- CHE109 – Engineering Chemistry: Basic chemistry for CSE

================================
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================

Q: Can I take CSE246 before completing CSE207?
A: No. CSE246 – Algorithms requires CSE207 – Data Structures.

Q: Does CSE302 have a lab component?
A: Yes. CSE302 – Database Systems is a 4.5-credit course with lab.

Q: Which major requires strong programming knowledge?
A: Intelligent Systems & Data Science (CSE366, CSE478, CSE474) and Software Engineering (CSE412, CSE430).

Q: Which major requires strong math background?
A: Data Science major, including courses like CSE303, CSE366, CSE478.

Q: Which major requires strong circuit knowledge?
A: Hardware Engineering major (CSE355, CSE442).

Q: What is the heaviest semester in 1st year?
A: Usually the 3rd semester with PHY109, MAT104, and CSE110.

Q: What is the lightest semester?
A: 4th semester with GEN226, STA102, and CSE200.

Q: What is considered the easiest major?
A: Most students find Software Engineering major easier than others.

Q: What is the hardest major?
A: Generally Data Science major, due to CSE303, CSE366, CSE478.

Q: Which major is most practical and hands-on?
A: Hardware Engineering major (CSE355, CSE442, CSE492).

Q: Which major is most research-focused?
A: Data Science major.

Q: Can I take more than one elective from a GED category?
A: Yes, but only one from each category counts toward required 9 credits.

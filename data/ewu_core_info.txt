About East West University (EWU)

## Overview
The idea of establishing a private university to provide quality education at an affordable cost in Bangladesh.

## Key Information
- Lead Founder: Dr. Mohammed Farashuddin
- Founding Organization: Progoti Foundation for Education and Development (PFED)
- Launch Year: 1996
- First Classes Started: September 1996
- Initial Faculty Members: 6
- Initial Students: 20
- Initial Campus Location: 43 Mohakhali C/A, Dhaka-1212
- Current Students: ~10,500 (Subject to change)

## Vision
- To augment national capacity for tertiary education and contribute to human capital development.
- To equip graduates with professional skills, innovative and critical thinking to take advantage of emerging opportunities.
- To provide access to affordable university-level education to less-well-to-do sections of society to raise employable skills and improve access to job opportunities.
- To be a top-notch learning center that combines excellence in education with strong human values and social responsibility.
- To promote a knowledge-based society and informed citizenry with respect for pluralism and democracy.

## Mission
- To become a center of excellence for higher education, making quality postsecondary education accessible at an affordable cost.
- To advance knowledge creation and promote economic, social and technological progress.
- To excel in critical disciplines that address socio-economic challenges and advance creative pursuits.
- To provide a stimulating learning environment where faculty and students can discover, critically examine, preserve and transmit knowledge, values, and wisdom.
- To ensure that students receive the highest quality of education, preparing them for a purposeful life, personally, professionally and socially.

## Eligibility Criteria
General Requirements:
- Minimum GPA 3.00 in both SSC and HSC.
- O-Level/A-Level: At least 4 B's in best 4 subjects (O-Level) and 3 C's in best 3 subjects (A-Level).

Program Specific Requirements:
- Engineering (CSE, ICE, EEE): Pass grades in Math & Physics.
- Civil Engineering: Math, Chemistry & Physics.
- Pharmacy: Aggregate GPA 8.00 (SSC+HSC); Min GPA 3.50 in Chemistry & Biology; Min 3.00 in Physics & Math.

4. Download Pay Slip / Pay Online (NexusPay, Rocket, bKash, etc.).

================================
SCHOLARSHIPS & FINANCIAL AID
================================

## Merit Scholarships
- Admission Top Scorers: Top performers in Admission Test (≥75% marks) get scholarships (continuation CGPA ≥3.50).
- Golden GPA 5.0 (SSC & HSC): 100% Tuition Waiver for 4 years (continuation CGPA ≥3.50).
- O/A Level High Achievers: 7 A's (O-Level) & 3 A's (A-Level) in one sitting. 100% Tuition Waiver.
- District Quota: Full tuition + lodging for one poor & meritorious student per district (GPA 5.0 in SSC/HSC).

## Financial Assistance (Need-cum-Merit)
Eligibility:
- Undergrad: Completed ≥12 credits, CGPA ≥2.60.
- Graduate: Completed ≥6 credits, CGPA ≥2.80.
- Sibling/Spouse Benefit: 50% waiver for second sibling/spouse studying simultaneously.
- Freedom Fighters: 3% quota with up to 100% waiver.

## Conditions
- No Dual Benefit: Cannot receive multiple scholarships.
- Retake Courses: Not covered by scholarship.
- Minimum Load: Must register for standard course load (Undergrad ≥9-12 credits).

## Application
- Apply through Financial Assistance Committee using prescribed forms.
- Selection based on Need-cum-Merit.

CODE OF CONDUCT
================================

## General Conduct Rules
Expected Behavior:
- Be of good moral character.
- Observe general rules of discipline, honesty, and punctuality.
- Show respect for the rights of others.
- Note: Willful violation seriously disturbs the academic environment.

ID Card Requirement:
- Students must display their photo identification cards on campus at all times for safety and security.

## Academic Misconduct
Viewed as a grave breach of discipline with zero tolerance.

Examples:
- Cheating at the examination by any method or means.
- Helping other students to cheat in examinations.
- Plagiarism: Reproducing the work of others as one's own.
- Fabrication or the falsification of any information with the intent to deceive.
- Forgery, alteration, or misuse of university documents or records.

Attendance Requirement:
- Students must attend a reasonable portion (not less than 80%) of the lectures, tutorials, and practical classes.

## Social Misconduct
Examples:
- Abusive or disorderly conduct.
- Sexual harassment.
- Physical assault in any form.
- Direct and indirect threat of violence.
- Verbal, mental, and physical harassment.
- Participation in any activity that may disrupt any function of the university.

## Health and Safety Violations
Examples:
- Smoking inside university buildings.
- Possession or use of alcoholic drinks and drugs.
- Possession of sharp weapons and firearms.

## Disciplinary Action & Academic Status
Probation and Dismissal:
- Undergraduate:
  - CGPA below 2.25: Probation.
  - CGPA below 1.0: Automatic dismissal (after first two or any subsequent semesters).
- Graduate:
  - CGPA below 2.60: Probation.
  - Failure to raise after two consecutive semesters: Dismissal.

General Penalties:
- Warning (written or verbal notice).
- Cancellation of the examination and/or an assignment.
- Expulsion for one or more semesters.
- Expulsion from the university (final dismissal).

Disciplinary Procedure:
- Allegations are examined by the Discipline Committee.
- Recommendations are made to the authority.
- Penalties are reflected in the student's Academic Transcripts/documents.

UNIVERSITY POLICIES
================================

## Sustainability & Environmental
Sustainability Climate Action Policy:
- Purpose: Integrate sustainability into all university operations.
- Key Actions: Green construction, reduce food waste, minimize plastics, expand green spaces.

Sustainable Procurement Policy:
- Purpose: Ensure sustainable, high-quality procurement at competitive cost.
- Key Principles: Align with climate goals, prefer sustainable suppliers, prioritize local sourcing.

Sustainable Investment Policy:
- Purpose: Guide responsible, ethical, ESG-aligned investments.
- Key Actions: No stock-market investment, avoid risky banks, support SDGs.

Carbon Emission Policy:
- Purpose: Reduce EWU's carbon footprint.
- Key Actions: Energy audits, green transportation, recycling systems, emission monitoring.

## Ethics & Social Responsibility
Equity, Diversity & Inclusion (EDI) Policy:
- Purpose: Promote a diverse, equitable, and inclusive university environment.
- Key Actions: Diversity targets, scholarships for underrepresented groups, curriculum review.

Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy:
- Purpose: Prevent bribery, corruption, and unethical practices.
- Key Principles: Zero tolerance, strict rules on gifts.

Outreach Projects Policy:
- Purpose: Build meaningful community partnerships.
- Key Actions: Mentoring, STEM outreach, disaster relief, environmental projects.

Social & Environmental Training Policy:
- Purpose: Promote environmental and social responsibility.
- Key Actions: Mandatory sustainability courses, student clubs, research grants.

## Academic & Technical
Disciplinary Policy:
- Purpose: Maintain standards of conduct for students and employees.
- Key Principles: Academic integrity, professionalism, confidentiality.

Plagiarism Policy:
- Purpose: Ensure academic honesty and originality.
- Actions: Use of plagiarism checking tools, strict accountability.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) Policy:
- Purpose: Guide ethical and permitted use of AI tools.
- Allowed: Brainstorming, grammar checking.
- Prohibited: Writing drafts, impersonation, misinformation, lack of citation.

Academic Grading & Evaluation Policy:
- Purpose: Define grading scale and academic rules.
- Scope: Undergraduate and graduate students.
- Key Areas: CGPA rules, probation/dismissal, retake policy, incomplete & withdrawal.

================================
SEXUAL HARASSMENT POLICY
================================

## Policy Stance
- Zero Tolerance Policy
- Sexual harassment is considered a grave breach of discipline and serious punishable status.

## Scope
Applies to all students, faculty, staff, applicants, and visitors. Covers acts on campus, in cyberspace, or where the perpetrator/victim is related to EWU.

## Prohibited Conduct
- Verbal: Teasing, abusive remarks, humiliation, stalking.
- Visual: Displaying sexually offensive photos, cartoons, or digital images.
- Behavioral: Indecent gestures, offensive behavior in class or campus.
- Cyber: Harassment via email, SMS, or social media.

## Complaint Process
1. Body: Sexual Harassment Complaint Committee (majority women).
2. Procedure: Prompt investigation with confidentiality maintained.
3. Timeline: Report submission within 30-60 working days.

## Penalties
- Students: Warning, cancellation of exams, suspension, or expulsion from the University.
- Faculty/Staff: Disciplinary action leading up to termination.
- False Complaints: Action will be taken against those filing intentionally false complaints.

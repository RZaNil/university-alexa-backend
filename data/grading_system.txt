East West University – Grading System & Academic Policies

East West University follows the standardized grading system recommended by the University Grants Commission (UGC) of Bangladesh, effectively adopted for all programs from Fall 2023.

================================
GRADING SCALE
================================

Grade A+ = 4.00 Grade Points (90-100 Marks) - Outstanding
Grade A  = 3.75 Grade Points (85-89 Marks) - Excellent
Grade A- = 3.50 Grade Points (80-84 Marks) - Very Good
Grade B+ = 3.25 Grade Points (75-79 Marks) - Good
Grade B  = 3.00 Grade Points (70-74 Marks) - Above Average
Grade B- = 2.75 Grade Points (65-69 Marks) - Average
Grade C+ = 2.50 Grade Points (60-64 Marks) - Below Average
Grade C  = 2.25 Grade Points (55-59 Marks) - Poor
Grade D  = 2.00 Grade Points (50-54 Marks) - Passing
Grade F  = 0.00 Grade Points (Below 50 Marks) - Failing

================================
SPECIAL GRADES
================================

Grade I (Incomplete):
- Must be cleared within next semester or becomes F
- Assigned when student satisfied most course requirements but could not complete a minor part due to valid, documented reasons

Grade W (Withdrawal):
- No impact on CGPA
- Appears on transcript as "W"
- Assigned when student officially withdraws after drop/add period but before final withdrawal deadline

Grade U (Unsatisfactory):
- Used for non-credit courses

================================
CALCULATION OF GPA AND CGPA
================================

Grade Point Average (GPA):
- Calculated for each semester
- Weighted average of grade points obtained in all courses attempted during that semester

Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA):
- Represents student's performance throughout their academic career
- Formula: Total Grade Points Earned / Total Credits Attempted

Credits Counting Rules:
- Credits Attempted: Courses with grades A+, A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+, C, D, and F are included
- Credits Earned: Courses with grades F, I, W, U do NOT count as earned credits towards graduation

================================
RETAKE & GRADE IMPROVEMENT POLICY
================================

Repeating Courses:
- If a student retakes a course, the grade obtained in the LAST ATTEMPT is used for calculating GPA and CGPA
- This applies regardless of whether the new grade is higher or lower

Transcript Record:
- Previous grades from earlier attempts will still appear on the official transcript
- Earlier attempts are NOT calculated into the CGPA

Eligibility for Retake:
- Students are typically allowed to retake courses where they earned a grade of C, D, or F
- Specific department rules may vary

================================
ACADEMIC STANDING (PROBATION & DISMISSAL)
================================

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS:
- Good Standing: CGPA ≥ 2.25
- Probation: CGPA between 1.00 and 2.24 (after first two semesters)
- Dismissal: Failure to raise CGPA to 2.25 after probation period (usually next two semesters)

GRADUATE PROGRAMS:
- Good Standing: CGPA ≥ 2.60
- Probation: CGPA below 2.60
- Dismissal: Failure to raise CGPA to 2.60 within limited time (e.g., two semesters)

================================
DEGREE REQUIREMENTS
================================

Minimum CGPA for Graduation:
- Undergraduate: 2.25
- Graduate: 2.60

Course Requirements:
- All core and required elective courses must be completed with a passing grade (D or higher)
- Courses with 'F', 'I', 'W', or 'U' do NOT count toward degree credit requirement

================================
MID-SEMESTER ASSESSMENT
================================

Typical Components:
- Midterm Exam: 20-30%
- Quizzes: 10-15%
- Assignments: 10-15%
- Attendance & Participation: 5-10%
- Final Exam: 40-50%

Note: Exact weights may vary by course and instructor.

================================
UGC COMPLIANCE
================================

This grading system ensures that:
- Academic transcripts from East West University are directly comparable to those from other UGC-compliant universities in Bangladesh
- Facilitates credit transfer between universities
- Supports applications for higher education opportunities nationally and internationally

================================
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================

Q: What is the minimum passing grade?
A: D (2.00) is the minimum passing grade for individual courses.

Q: Can I improve my grade by retaking a course?
A: Yes, but only the last attempt grade counts toward CGPA, even if it's lower.

Q: What happens if I get an 'I' grade?
A: You must complete the missing work within the next semester, or it becomes an 'F'.

Q: How many times can I retake a course?
A: Generally, you can retake courses with C, D, or F grades. Check with your department for specific limits.

Q: What CGPA do I need to graduate?
A: Undergraduate: 2.25 minimum. Graduate: 2.60 minimum.

Q: Does 'W' grade affect my CGPA?
A: No, 'W' does not affect CGPA but appears on your transcript.

Q: What is a good CGPA?
A: A CGPA of 3.50 or above is considered very good. CGPA of 3.75+ is excellent.

Q: How is CGPA calculated?
A: CGPA = Total Grade Points Earned / Total Credits Attempted. Only graded courses count.

Q: What grade do I need to maintain my scholarship?
A: Most merit scholarships require maintaining a CGPA of 3.50 or higher.

Q: Can I drop a course without it affecting my CGPA?
A: Yes, if you withdraw before the deadline, you get a 'W' grade which doesn't affect CGPA.
